6. Thurgau - Groot/Grat and Dreil√§nderstein

The first is the Dreil√§nderstein, at the southernmost point of Thurgau where it meets Z√ºrich and St. Gallen at a triple point. I didn't find a special stone here, just a trail sign with the elevation (992 m), some stone border markers, and a small restaurant with people eating.

According to this person on Hikr, this is a mere 991.3 m and the Dreil√§nderstein point is 992 m. 9. Aargau - Geissfluegrat

November 9, 2024

At a little over 900m, this is one of the lowest high points. In fact (according to trivia from my partner's coworkers) there only two cantons in Switzerland without a ski lift - I guessed this answer by knowing that only Geneva and Basel-Stadt have lower highpoints than Aargau.

11. Schaffhausen - Hage

January 19, 2025

Schaffhausen is Switzerland's northernmost canton. It has three connected components, the largest surrounded almost completely by Germany. The train there from Zurich actually goes through Germany! Its has the 4th lowest cantonal highoint at 912 m, just beating out Aargau.

My initial attempt was a snowy run on 2025-11-23, made more challenging by not paying attention very well to the route I intended to follow. I didn't tag the actual summit, assuming the Hagenturm tower stood there and not checking the topo carefully on my phone because the battery was almost dead in the cold, and I hadn't done my due diligence beforehand, having changed my plans last minute due to the weather. Lesson learned (the second time, after making the same mistake in Aargau..) - the technically easy ones are often easy to miss! The actual summit is 3.5m higher and 93m to the north of the tower.
