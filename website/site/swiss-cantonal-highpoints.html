Swiss Cantonal Highpoints

6/26 Cantons

2023-11-01

Updated 2024-05-01

I recently perused my favorite website, and was curious about the list of the highest points in each canton (Federal state) in Switzerland.

The 26 cantons have a wide variety of highpoints, all in an area one tenth the size of my home state of California. This includes some serious mountains requiring specific conditions and climbing skills, but also many easily runnable or bikeable points in the lower regions.

As of August 2023, before officially starting this project, I had already climbed 4 and had passed by a few others. It was fun to look back through my photos to create this initial post, which I'll update as I climb more.

6. Thurgau - Groot/Grat and Dreiländerstein

May 01, 2024

This was a quick 15k springtime run/hike over the nice fire roads and forested hiking trails in the Züri Oberland, between the Steg im Tösstal and Bauma train stations. As is common for the lower-lying cantons, the highpoint is near the border. The Zürich highpoint is also nearby and would be logical to tag at the same time (but I had already been there).

As is also common for highpoints on borders, it's not always trivial to specify the highpoint. Both contenders are on the border with St. Gallen, and I visited both, hiking over the Hörnli on the way in, which had the best views on the route.

The first is the Dreiländerstein, a triple point where St. Gallen, Thurgau, and Zürich meet. I was a bit disappointed to not find a special stone here, just a trail sign with the elevation (992m), some stone border markers, and small restaurant with people eating.

The second is a mountain/hill named both "Groot" and "Grat" on various trail signs along the way ("Grat" means "ridge" in high German and presumably "Groot", the official name on the Swiss map since 2002, is a version of the same word in a local dialect). The summit is 996m, but is just inside Sankt Gallen, not on the border. There is a stone (maybe) marking the highpoint of Thurgau. Acccording to this person on hikr, this is mere 922.1m and the Dreiländerstein point is 992.2-9m. Those are close enough to likely be within the error bounds of such measurements, but fortunately it's very easy to visit both points.
